Yes, I'm a slacker with blogging. But that doesn't mean we quit the most expensive hobby. Here's a list of where things stand:

1. This has been a very successful year with honey and lip balm sales. We've really gotten to know more of our neighborhood, which is awesome.

2. The lip balm container problem may finally be resolved (more on that in another post).

3. Mighty Mite Thermal Treatment is our current approach for dealing with varroa mites.

4. We went into winter with 6 hives and entered spring with 3.

5. We did another on-site presentation with neighborhood children.

6. We attended the annual Geneva Bee Conference.

7. Two more packages were purchased — we briefly had 5 hives.

8. One hive that swarmed actually absconded entirely.

9. With 4 hives left, another swarmed — much too high to catch.

10. For the first time, we purchased a mated queen.

11. With 10 queen cells brewing as potential replacements, we attempted several hive splits.

12. More than anything, we really want to figure out queen rearing. It's expensive but fascinating.

13. We've decided to enroll in the eCornell Master Beekeeper program.

**Interesting fact:** Our hive that absconded was the last of the mean-queen descendants. We're not sorry to see them go.
