title: Lip Balm Container Annoyances
date: "2018-07-20"
excerpt: Nine methods, countless spills, and one clear winner for labels. The full saga of figuring out how to actually package homemade lip balm.
---

Putting together lip balm has been a frustrating process. Here's every method we've tried:

**Method 1: Mann Lake tubes with beaker pouring**
Messy spills, knocked-over containers, slow, and a sunken appearance on the finished product.

**Method 2: Mann Lake tubes with pipette**
Fewer spills, but still slow. Pipettes became unusable after minimal use as the wax solidified inside them.

**Method 3: Lip Balm Filling Tray from Bulk Apothecary**
Mann Lake tubes didn't fit the tray — had to switch to Milliard brand tubes.

**Method 4: Bulk Apothecary tray + Milliard tubes + Onlinelabels.com labels**
A breakthrough. Fill 50 tubes at once, overfill slightly, scrape flat. Minor issues: slight counter mess and loose-fitting caps that let in dust.

**Method 5: Milliard tubes with Milliard brand labels**
The labels have a tab meant to secure caps but fail to wrap completely around the tube. Wasteful.

**Method 6: Milliard tubes, second batch**
Despite a manufacturer replacement, caps remained excessively loose. They fall off when inverted.

**Method 7: California Home Goods Crafting Kit + tamper evident labels from Onlinelabels.com**
Excellent fit, minimal loose caps, tamper evident labels worked great. One catch: the manufacturer doesn't sell replacement containers, even though they claim compatibility with standard-sized tubes.

**Method 8: Pure Acres Farm containers**
Fit neither tray. Revisiting pipette method.

**Method 9: Premium Vials containers**
Awaiting arrival. TBD.

**Our firm recommendation:** [Onlinelabels.com](https://www.onlinelabels.com) for lip balm labels. No contest.
