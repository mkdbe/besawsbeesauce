---
title: But, is it Organic?
date: "2018-05-13"
excerpt: The most common question we get — and the honest answer is no. Here's why urban honey can't be certified organic, and why that doesn't make it any less good.
---

*"Is the honey organic?"*

Nope. Let me explain.

Honeybees can travel up to 5 miles to gather pollen and nectar. Right now, the girls are all about the dandelions. Are the dandelions in your yard certified organic? Is the soil organic? Maybe your yard is — but what about your neighbors'?

We have no idea where they've been.

For a honey operation to be certified organic, hives need to be placed at the center of an at least 5-mile radius of **certified organic land**. Every farm, yard, and field within flying range would need to be chemical-free.

We live in the city in Rochester, NY. We cannot prove that everyone's yard is free from chemicals, herbicides, and pesticides. Harvesting certifiable organic honey here is impossible.

So when you see urban honey labeled "organic" — the business is either misinformed or not being straight with you. Raw and local? Absolutely. Organic? Prove it.

**Interesting fact:** If you buy raw, local honey that also claims to be certified organic, ask where their hives are placed. If the answer isn't "the middle of thousands of acres of certified organic farmland," take the label with a grain of salt.
