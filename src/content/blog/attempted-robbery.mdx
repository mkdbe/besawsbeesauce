The ladies were in full force fighting off robbers last weekend. As the hives prepare for winter, they have absolutely no patience for intruders trying to steal their honey stores.

Late fall is prime time for robbing behavior. Other insects — including bees from weaker hives and wasps — will attempt to raid a colony when natural nectar sources dry up. A strong hive will post guards and eject anyone who doesn't belong.

**Interesting fact:** Bees tend to rob weaker hives because they're easier targets. A healthy, strong colony is much harder to break into — another reason keeping your hives strong matters going into winter.
