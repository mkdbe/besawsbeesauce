It's amazing how quickly winter weather can turn into spring. We've been rushing around with bee-related things and are finally catching our breath long enough to give an update.

## Winter Survival

Every few weeks during winter, I place my ear directly on a hive and listen for activity — usually a consistent humming sound when things are going well. By February, we had lost most of what we went in with. One hive was weak to begin with; two others had tipped backward over the winter and likely died from excessive moisture when the snow melted.

Moldy frames. Very sad.

To prevent this going forward, we're adding topsoil to give the hives a very slight forward incline so water drains away from the hive.

## New Bees

We enjoyed our Carniolan bees so much last year (even though they died) that we ordered more from Hungry Bear Farms. We got notification on April 5th with a letter telling us not to panic about installing them in winter-ish weather.

Bees typically won't leave the hive below 45°F and can't fly below 55°F. It was in the low 30s and snowing on April 7th. We felt too bad about freezing them in the shipping box, so we installed that afternoon when it climbed to the mid-40s.

We moved them into a nuc box to help with insulation during the cold snap, added foam as an entrance reducer, and gave all the living hives sugar patties to keep everyone fed.

We now have 3 living hives going into the season. 

**Interesting fact:** In winter, bees form a tight cluster around the queen and keep the inside of the hive between 81–95°F regardless of the outside temperature.
