## The Swarms We Could Never Catch

See the top of that tree? That's how high they went. Every. Time.

For the first swarm, we called Chris Veazey — who climbs trees for a living. He graciously climbed a pine tree covered in poison ivy with no bee jacket. Alas, they re-swarmed across the street while he was attempting to remove them. Huge kudos for the effort.

We had a total of 7 swarms (that we know of) and every single one went completely out of reach. While swarms are a "good" and natural thing, it didn't help us much. We replaced 3 queens, but the hives weren't able to recover.

## A New Allergy

Dan has had some ridiculous reactions to bee stings — especially if they got him in the foot. It would turn red and swell up for days. On one special occasion, while mowing the lawn, he got attacked. His face turned red, he had trouble breathing, and he was sick on the way to our thankfully very close doctor's office. He had his first ambulance ride to the emergency room at Strong where it was confirmed he was in anaphylaxis.

I was ready to quit beekeeping — because I prefer he not die — but instead he started going to an immunologist for weekly bee venom injections. These are 97% effective in preventing allergic reactions and are now reduced to once a month.

## 2019 By the Numbers

We extracted about 10 gallons of honey in July and absolutely none in the fall. The hives were so weak from swarming that we combined everything down to 4. We treated for mites, made sure they had enough food for winter, and closed shop for the season.

## 2020 So Far

We gave them sugar patties and hoped for the best. A month later we were down to 2 hives. A week after that, 1 hive. And then we lost that one too.

This is the first year since my very first season that we lost 100% of our colonies. Three appear to have died from mites, one from humidity and mold.

Some positives from the wreckage:

- We completed our first eCornell Master Beekeeping course
- We sold out of honey thanks to our amazing neighbors
- Without honey to sell, we expanded into lip balm and bath bombs
- We've made some new friends along the way
- New bees are arriving soon

**Interesting fact:** Our amazing health insurance is the only reason we can afford both immunotherapy and EpiPens — and therefore continue beekeeping.
